Terrace/Balcony



General Tips for Balcony Gardening


Different vegetables require different pot sizes and sunlight conditions. Make sure to select the right combination for your terrace or balcony garden.

Container Selection:
- Ensure the pots have good drainage holes to prevent waterlogging.
- Use pots that are deep and wide enough to accommodate the root systems.
- A minimum depth of 12 inches is usually sufficient for most vegetables.
- Choose containers made of durable materials like plastic, terracotta, or fabric grow bags.

Soil:
- Use high-quality potting soil that is well-draining and rich in organic matter.
- Avoid using garden soil, which can be too dense and may carry pests and diseases.

Watering:
- Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
- Watering frequency may vary based on weather conditions and plant needs.
- Ensure even watering to avoid dry spots or overly saturated areas.

Sunlight:
- Place the pots in a location that receives ample sunlight.
- Most vegetables, including onions, chili peppers, and tomatoes, require at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily.

Fertilizers & Manures:
- Use balanced, slow-release organic fertilizers or liquid fertilizers as per the plant's growth stages.
- Regularly check the soil nutrient levels and replenish as needed.
